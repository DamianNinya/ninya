Prowords (Procedure Words) are standardized words and phrases used to ensure clarity and brevity in military communications. Below is a list of essential prowords used in NATO and allied forces' communications:

Pro Tip: Prowords are key to ensuring effective and fast communication, especially during high-pressure or combat situations. When in doubt, use prowords to maintain clarity and avoid ambiguity.

Term Meaning Usage/Context
Actual Unit leader speaking personally e.g., "Alpha Actual" indicates the commanding officer is on the line.
Affirmative Yes Used to confirm instructions or statements.
Copy Message received Acknowledges that the transmission has been understood.
How Copy Request for confirmation Inquires if the message was received clearly ("How copy?").
Over End of transmission (reply expected) Indicates the speaker has finished and is awaiting a response.
Out End of communication Signifies the conversation is concluded; no reply expected.
Oscar Mike On the Move Used when units are relocating.
Bravo Zulu Well Done A commendation for a job well executed.
Checkfire Warning of enemy fire Alerts friendly forces of incoming or near-miss enemy rounds.
Fox One Missile launch (semi-active guidance) Specifies launch of a semi-active radar-guided missile.
Fox Two Missile launch (infrared-guided) Denotes launch of an infrared-guided missile.
Fox Three Missile launch (active radar-guided) Indicates a launch of an active radar-guided missile.
Wilco Will Comply Acknowledges receipt of instructions and confirms compliance.
Standby Wait for further instructions Used when a pause in communications is needed until new orders are given.
Roger Message received and understood Commonly used to confirm clear reception of a transmission.
Say Again Repeat the message Request for the speaker to repeat the message due to poor reception.
