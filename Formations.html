1. Linear Formation

The linear formation is a straight-line movement pattern used for open terrain. It ensures that firepower is maximized in the forward direction. The roles in this formation are as follows:

  • The lead man keeps eyes front.
  • The second man looks left or right.
  • The third man looks in the opposite direction of the second man.
  • The fourth man watches the rear while staying aware of surroundings.
  • The fifth man provides additional support and covers any blind spots.
  • The last man always watches the back to prevent ambushes.

2. Staggered Formation

The staggered formation is similar to the linear formation but with a zig-zag pattern. This formation improves flexibility and provides overlapping fields of fire. The positioning works as follows:

  • Each person is slightly offset from the person in front of them.
  • This staggered alignment increases visibility and reaction time to threats.
  • The last man maintains rear security while staying offset.

3. Diamond Formation

The diamond formation is used when all-around security is needed, especially in high-risk areas. The team is positioned as follows:

  • One person at the front (point man).
  • Two individuals to the left and right to cover the flanks.
  • The last two individuals cover the rear, ensuring full 360-degree security.

This formation allows for quick response to threats from any direction and is effective for controlled movements in uncertain environments.
